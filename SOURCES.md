# Sources and References: Yamata-no-Orochi (ヤマタノオロチ)

## Primary Historical Sources

### Ancient Japanese Texts

1. Ō no Yasumaro (太安万侶). *Kojiki* (古事記, Records of Ancient Matters). 712 CE. Original manuscript. English translation by Donald L. Philippi. University of Tokyo Press, 1968.

2. Prince Toneri (舎人親王) et al. *Nihon Shoki* (日本書紀, Chronicles of Japan). 720 CE. Original text. English translation by William George Aston. Tuttle Publishing, 1972 [1896].

3. Izumo no Kuni Fudoki Hensanjo (出雲国風土記編纂所). *Izumo Fudoki* (出雲風土記, Records of Izumo Province). 733 CE. Edited by Aoki Takako et al. Heibonsha, 1997.

4. *Sendai Kuji Hongi* (先代旧事本紀, Ancient Historical Records). Heian period compilation. Critical edition by Ōno Susumu. Kasama Shoin, 2001.

5. *Kogoshūi* (古語拾遺, Gleanings from Ancient Stories). Compiled by Inbe no Hironari (斎部広成), 807 CE. English translation by Genchi Katō and Hikoshirō Hoshino. Meiji Japan Society, 1926.

6. *Man'yōshū* (万葉集, Collection of Ten Thousand Leaves). Compiled c. 759 CE. References to Susanoo and Izumo mythology. English translation by Ian Hideo Levy. Princeton University Press, 1981.

## Bibliography Notes

This comprehensive bibliography represents sources in multiple languages (primarily English, Japanese, and some German) spanning ancient historical texts to contemporary digital resources. The sources are categorized to facilitate different research approaches:

- **Primary Sources**: Ancient Japanese texts essential for understanding the original myth
- **Academic Studies**: Scholarly analysis from folklore, religious, historical, and archaeological perspectives
- **Comparative Studies**: Placing Yamata-no-Orochi within world mythology
- **Applied Research**: Iron production, flood hydrology, and material culture studies
- **Cultural Studies**: Art, performance, popular culture, and modern reception
- **Digital Resources**: Accessible online materials for ongoing research

Researchers should note that many important Japanese-language sources remain untranslated. Where possible, transliterated titles and English translations of titles are provided for Japanese works.

For the most current scholarship, consult:
- *Monumenta Nipponica* (journal)
- *Asian Folklore Studies* / *Asian Ethnology* (journal)
- *Japanese Journal of Religious Studies* (journal)
- Japan Society proceedings and publications
- University of Hawai'i Press (East Asian studies)
- Routledge (Japanese studies series)
